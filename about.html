About Cloud Computing

Cloud computing is the delivery of on-demand computing services — from servers and storage to databases, networking, software, and intelligence — over the internet to offer faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale.

What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing eliminates the need for companies to procure, configure, or manage infrastructure themselves, and instead pay for what they use on demand. Think of it as renting a supercomputer's power rather than buying one — available any time, from anywhere in the world.

AWS (Amazon Web Services) is the world's most comprehensive and broadly adopted cloud platform, offering over 200 fully featured services from data centers globally.

⚡

On-Demand Self-Service

Provision computing capabilities — server time, network storage — automatically without requiring human interaction with each service provider.

🌐

Broad Network Access

Capabilities are available over the network and accessed through standard mechanisms — mobile phones, tablets, laptops, and workstations.

🏛

Resource Pooling

Provider's computing resources are pooled to serve multiple consumers using a multi-tenant model, dynamically assigned and reassigned as needed.

📈

Rapid Elasticity

Capabilities can be elastically provisioned and released to scale rapidly outward and inward commensurate with demand.

📊

Measured Service

Cloud systems automatically control and optimize resource use by leveraging a metering capability — pay only for what you use.

🔒

Security by Design

AWS provides 300+ security, compliance, and governance services with encryption, identity management, and threat detection built in.

Cloud Service Models
🏢

Infrastructure

IaaS

Access virtualized computing, storage, and networking resources over the internet. AWS EC2, S3, and VPC are classic IaaS examples.

🛠

Platform

PaaS

Development tools, databases, and middleware delivered over the cloud, enabling developers to build and deploy apps without managing infrastructure.

💻

Software

SaaS

Fully functional applications delivered over the internet on a subscription basis — Gmail, Office 365, Salesforce, and more.

Key Benefits of Cloud Computing

Cost Efficiency

Trade capital expense for variable expense. No upfront hardware investment — pay only for what you consume and reduce total cost of ownership.

Global Reach in Minutes

Deploy your application in multiple regions around the world with just a few clicks and low latency for your customers.

Massive Scalability

Scale up or down instantly based on demand — handle millions of users during peak traffic without pre-provisioning capacity.

Speed & Agility

New IT resources are a click away — reduce time to provision from weeks to minutes and dramatically increase developer productivity.

Disaster Recovery

Multi-AZ and multi-region architectures ensure business continuity with automated backups and geo-redundant storage.

Security & Compliance

AWS meets the most rigorous security standards — HIPAA, PCI-DSS, SOC 1/2/3, ISO 27001, and hundreds more compliance programs.

Core AWS Services
⚙

EC2

Elastic compute instances — virtual servers in the cloud with full OS control.

🗃

S3

Object storage with 99.999999999% durability — the backbone of cloud storage.

🔌

Lambda

Serverless compute — run code without provisioning servers, pay per invocation.

📊

RDS

Managed relational databases — MySQL, PostgreSQL, Oracle, and more.

🌐

CloudFront

Global CDN delivering content with low latency from 400+ edge locations.

🔒

IAM

Identity & access management — control who can access what in your cloud.

🔸

VPC

Virtual private cloud — logically isolated section of the AWS network.

🤖

SageMaker

Fully managed ML platform to build, train, and deploy AI models at scale.
